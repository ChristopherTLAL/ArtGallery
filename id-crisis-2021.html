I'm Not Gay, I'm Just a Sissy

The series explores the idea of 'sissy' as a performance of femininity, and the ways in which this performance can be both liberating and constricting.

The drag queen in the first artwork is shown in a moment of self-reflection, gazing at her own reflection in the mirror. She is both the subject and the object of her own gaze, and the viewer is invited to share in this intimate moment.

The painting in the second artwork shows the drag queen in a more playful mood. This celebratory image represents the joy and freedom that can be found in embracing one's femininity.

However, the third artwork shows the drag queen in a more emotional state. It captures the vulnerability of someone who is constantly performing a role that is often marginalised. It highlights the ways in which the 'sissy' is often ridiculed and misunderstood, and how this can lead to a feeling of isolation and loneliness.

No Homo

Two artworks were presented here for the topic 'No Homo' which uses a slang term typically used by straight men to reassure each other that they are not gay.

In the first painting, a conservative old male stares at you, showing his privilege as a man. Echoing the 'big nose, big hose' belief, I made the character have a penis-like nose. It reveals how the men in society, with their religious passion for male genitals, stare at the queer community.

The second painting shows how the queer community was rejected by the so-called mainstream community. The different colours were used to show how these people were not seen as human beings, and how they were discriminated against.
